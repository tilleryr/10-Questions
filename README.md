# 10 Questions

A voice-based mock interview simulator powered by Google Gemini AI. You paste a job description, and the app conducts a realistic 10-question conversational interview with a hiring manager who adapts questions to the role, asks follow-ups, and pushes you to go deeper. After the interview, it scores each answer on five criteria and delivers a career coach-style performance report with specific advice on how to improve.

## How It Works

1. Open the app in Chrome (voice features require Chrome).
2. Paste your free Gemini API key. Get one in under 2 minutes at [aistudio.google.com/apikey](https://aistudio.google.com/apikey). No credit card needed.
3. Paste a job description and enter the position level (e.g., COO, VP of Operations).
4. Optionally paste your resume so the interviewer can ask targeted questions.
5. Click "Enter Interview Room" and speak your answers out loud. A type-instead option is available.
6. After 10 questions and a closing Q&A, the app generates a performance scorecard.

## Scorecard Criteria

Each of the 10 questions is scored on a 1–5 scale across five criteria:

- **Responsiveness** — Did you answer the actual question?
- **Clarity** — Were your words clear and easy to follow?
- **Relevance** — Was your answer relevant to the job?
- **Seniority Fit** — Was your response appropriate for the level you're targeting?
- **Recovery** — If you stumbled, did you recover?

Total possible score: 250 points. Each question includes specific coaching advice for any non-perfect scores.

## What You Need

- Google Chrome (for voice input/output)
- A free Google Gemini API key
- A job description to practice with

## What's Coming

This is a work in progress. Planned additions include a recruiter screening mode, a panel interview mode with multiple distinct interviewers, progress tracking across sessions, and additional scoring analytics.

## Privacy

Your API key is entered in the browser and sent directly to Google's Gemini API. It is never stored or transmitted anywhere else. Your interview transcripts exist only in your browser session and disappear when you close the page.
